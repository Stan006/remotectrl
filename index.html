FAQ

What permissions does the Android app use, and why?

Network: to connect to your PC over Wi‑Fi and exchange commands, stats and screenshots. No external servers involved.

Storage (optional): only when you initiate file transfer. The app reads the file you select and sends it to your PC. It never scans your storage.

Why does Windows Defender flag the listener as a “virus”?

It’s a false positive common to remote‑control tools. The listener is small, unsigned, accepts TCP connections, can simulate keyboard/mouse, send screenshots and receive files — behaviors that resemble malware. The key differences: no persistence, no registry hacks, no telemetry, no cloud, local‑only by default, and you run it explicitly.

You can restore and allow it in Windows Security, then add it to Exclusions to prevent future removals.

Can anyone else access my PC?

Only devices on your network that know your PC’s IP and port. RemotePC is LAN‑first and not reachable from the internet unless you manually port‑forward (not recommended).

Does the app track me or send data to the cloud?

No. No accounts, no analytics, no telemetry. Traffic stays in your network.

Can I use it over mobile data?

Possible with manual port forwarding and a stable public IP, but this is an advanced, optional setup.
